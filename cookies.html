What Cookies Are

Cookies are small data files placed on your device when you visit a website. These files allow a website to recognize a device, store certain information about user interactions, and enhance operational functionality. Cookies do not typically contain directly identifiable personal information; however, in certain circumstances, they may be associated with information that could be considered personal data under applicable data protection regulations.

Cookies may operate as session-based technologies that expire automatically when a browser session ends, or as persistent technologies that remain on a device until deleted manually or automatically after a defined expiration period.

In addition to traditional HTTP cookies, similar technologies may be used, including web beacons, tracking pixels, embedded scripts, local storage objects, and device fingerprinting mechanisms. For purposes of this policy, all such technologies are collectively referred to as “cookies.”

Purpose of Cookie Usage

The use of cookies at FIRST AID INTERNATIONAL, INC. is structured around legitimate operational needs, user experience optimization, digital security, communication effectiveness, and performance measurement.

Cookies are implemented to ensure that our website functions as intended. Without essential cookies, key services such as secure donation processing, volunteer registration, training enrollment, and contact form submission may not operate correctly.

Cookies also facilitate technical infrastructure management, including session continuity, server load distribution, fraud detection, and data encryption verification. These mechanisms are critical to safeguarding sensitive information related to donors, volunteers, beneficiaries, and partners.

Analytical cookies are deployed to measure user engagement patterns. By analyzing anonymized or aggregated interaction data, we can evaluate which educational resources are most accessed, which regions demonstrate increased interest in first aid training, and which outreach campaigns generate measurable impact. This information supports strategic decision-making and enhances the delivery of life-saving educational materials.

Functional cookies allow us to remember user preferences, including language settings, accessibility configurations, consent selections, and interface customization. These preferences contribute to a more inclusive and accessible digital environment.

Communication-related cookies may be associated with newsletter subscription systems, campaign performance metrics, event registration tools, and embedded multimedia content. Such cookies assist in assessing outreach effectiveness while maintaining data minimization principles.

Security cookies are employed to detect suspicious behavior, mitigate malicious traffic, prevent cross-site request forgery attacks, and monitor irregular access attempts. These measures are fundamental to preserving website integrity and protecting users from unauthorized data access.

Categories of Cookies

Cookies used by FIRST AID INTERNATIONAL, INC. generally fall into the following categories.

  • Strictly necessary cookies are indispensable for core website functionality. These cookies support authentication, system stability, and transaction security. Because they are essential to technical operation, they may not require explicit consent under certain legal frameworks.
  • Performance and analytics cookies collect information regarding website usage patterns. This may include page views, navigation pathways, bounce rates, browser types, operating systems, and device classifications. Such data is typically anonymized and aggregated to prevent individual identification.
  • Functional cookies enhance user experience by remembering selected preferences and accessibility settings. These cookies enable consistent interaction across sessions and improve usability for returning visitors.
  • Third-party cookies may be deployed through integrated services such as payment processors, event management platforms, cloud hosting providers, analytics vendors, or social media plugins. These cookies are governed by the respective third-party privacy policies and may operate independently of our direct control.
  • Marketing or outreach cookies may measure the effectiveness of public awareness campaigns or donation initiatives. FIRST AID INTERNATIONAL, INC. does not engage in invasive behavioral advertising practices inconsistent with its humanitarian mission; however, certain outreach performance tools may rely on limited tracking mechanisms to evaluate engagement impact.

Legal Basis for Processing

The legal basis for cookie deployment depends on jurisdiction and cookie classification.

Where required under regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the ePrivacy Directive, or comparable data protection laws, non-essential cookies are deployed only after obtaining valid user consent. Consent mechanisms are designed to be granular, freely given, informed, and revocable at any time.

In certain circumstances, cookie usage may rely on legitimate interest, particularly where cookies are necessary for fraud prevention, cybersecurity protection, or essential technical functionality. Such legitimate interests are balanced against individual rights and freedoms through internal assessment procedures.

Contractual necessity may serve as a legal basis where cookies are essential to provide a requested service, such as completing a donation transaction or registering for training.

Compliance with legal obligations may also require limited cookie deployment in order to meet regulatory security standards.

Data Minimization and Purpose Limitation

FIRST AID INTERNATIONAL, INC. adheres to strict data minimization principles. Cookies are configured to collect only the data necessary for their intended purpose. We do not intentionally deploy cookies designed to collect excessive or intrusive personal information.

Data collected through cookies is processed exclusively for specified and legitimate purposes, including service improvement, operational continuity, cybersecurity protection, and communication effectiveness assessment.

We do not sell personal data collected through cookies to third parties.

Retention Period

The lifespan of cookies varies depending on function and necessity.

  • Session cookies expire automatically when the browser session ends.
  • Persistent cookies may remain active for short durations such as thirty to ninety days when related to temporary preferences or campaign measurement.
  • Certain functional cookies may persist for up to twelve or twenty-four months when required to maintain user experience continuity.
  • Security-related cookies may have shorter durations aligned with risk mitigation protocols.

All retention periods are subject to periodic review to ensure proportionality and regulatory compliance.

International Data Transfers

As an international humanitarian organization, FIRST AID INTERNATIONAL, INC. may operate digital infrastructure across multiple jurisdictions. Cookie-related data may therefore be processed in countries outside a user’s country of residence.

When cross-border data transfers occur, appropriate safeguards are implemented. These may include standard contractual clauses, data processing agreements, adequacy decisions recognized by competent authorities, and encryption protocols.

We assess cross-border processing risks to ensure continued protection consistent with applicable data protection laws.

User Rights and Controls

Users maintain full autonomy over cookie preferences.

Upon initial website access, a consent management interface provides options to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customize preferences by category.

Users may modify consent settings at any time through the website’s privacy settings panel.

Additionally, most web browsers permit users to block, restrict, or delete cookies directly through browser configuration settings. Instructions vary by browser and are available through official support documentation.

Disabling certain cookies may limit website functionality, particularly with respect to secure transactions and interactive features.

Some browsers transmit “Do Not Track” signals; however, universal implementation standards for these signals remain inconsistent across digital ecosystems.
